# How to launch an ICBM?

### Prerequisites:

  - At least the `ICBMs` technology has been researched
  - A fully constructed rocket in either a rocket-silo or a ballistic rocket-silo
  - At least one payload-vehicle (or valid warhead if using the `Legacy launch system` setting), loaded into the above rocket-silo rocket
  - Something that offends you, i.e. biters or pentapods (something to target, in seriousness)

## Manual:

If the above prerequisites are ready, either manually select the target destination with the `ICBM-remote` (available as a shortcut after researching the `ICBMs` technology, or from pressing `ALT + SHIFT + T` (default hotkey, configurable)).

## Circuit:

If the above prerequisites are ready, connect the circuit-network direcly to the rocket-silo (ballistic or otherwise). Once connected, a new GUI window should appear to the right of the default rocket-silo GUI; this new window should list the following signals:

  - ### Regular rocket-silos **cannot target different surfaces** than the one they are on (outside of "orbit" surfaces) by default. Can be toggled via a runtime setting.

  - `Launch`: Defines which signal indicates a launch should be attempted (default of 'green-check' signal)
  - `X`: Defines which signal indicates the **X** coordinate of the target (default of 'X' signal)
  - `Y`: Defines which signal indicates the **Y** coordinate of the target (default of 'Y' signal)
  - `I`: (For multi-surface strikes) Defines which signal indicates the **surface index**  - determines the surface to target (default of of 'I' signal)
    - The needed `I` value for a given surface can be determined from the value listed next the the surface/planet's name available from the dropdown list in the GUI window


## From Google's abominable intelligence, each dedicated warhead and when to use them:

1. Jericho: The "Frontline Workhorse" (Gleba)

  - **Best** For: Aggressive expansion and clearing large nests on a budget.
  - **Why**: Since the final product doesn't spoil but the ingredients do, you are incentivized to over-produce and over-use them. They are the "cheapest" high-tier weapon once your Gleba infrastructure is set up.
  - **Situation**: When you need to saturate a massive area with explosives and don't want to burn through precious Uranium or Tungsten.

2. Rod from God: The "Planet-Cracker" (Vulcanus)

  - **Best** For: Surgical strikes on high-HP targets or clearing space for mega-bases.
  - **Why**: 2,500 Tungsten plates represent massive physical density. This isn't an explosion; it's a kinetic impact. It’s perfect for targets that might have high resistances to fire or acid but can't handle raw physical force.
  - **Situation**: Deep-vein biter nests or "cleaning" a landing zone on a new planet where you have plenty of storage space but limited specialized assembly.

3. Atomic Warhead: The "Strategic Deterrent" (Nauvis)

  - **Best** For: Late-game "Panic Button" or clearing Nauvis/Aquilo.
  - **Why**: With a cost of 50 RCUs and 271 U-235, these are painfully expensive to lose. You don't fire these at small clusters.
  - **Situation**: Total annihilation of a sector where you need the lingering radiation to ensure nothing survives or resettles immediately. Use these when you have a surplus of U-235 from Kovarex and a solid RCU line.

4. Mjölnir: The "Elite Guardian" (Fulgora)

  - **Best** For: High-tech defense and "clean" kills.
  - **Why**: It utilizes Fulgora’s lightning and electromagnetic tech. Because the PUs are a byproduct of scrap, your Mjölnir supply is tied to your factory's general activity.
  - **Situation**: Defending high-value outposts or clearing targets where you want to avoid radioactive fallout (Atomic) or massive cratering (Rods). It’s the "scalpel" of the high-tier nukes.

5. Standard Atomic Bomb: The "Tactical Nudge" (Nauvis)

  - **Best** For: Early-to-mid game "clearance" and personal defense.
  - **Why**: It lacks the RCU requirement of the modular warheads, making it much faster to hand-craft or automate early on.
  - **Situation**: When a nest is just slightly too big for your tank, but doesn't justify the logistical effort of a Ballistic Rocket launch.
